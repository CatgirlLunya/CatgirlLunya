(super) metroid | updated 9/15/2024

i don't play much super metroid anymore, but i used to put a ton of time into it, and i've probably played over 100 randomizers. although it's a short game(3-6 hour casually, 40 minutes to an hour competitively) the tight movement, music, controls, unique abilities, and story all make it one of my favorite games ever. on top of randomizers(i played at expert level on VARIA on average) which test how well you can do very situational tech depending on what items it places where, i also speedran alot, getting a 41 minute in game time i believe as my best. maybe not very good, but placing in the top 50% of a really competitive game as a 15 year old made me pretty satisfied. one day i may come back now that i'm actually good at video games but who knows.

academics

math | updated 9/15/2024

i've always really loved math, but for the past 2 years or so i've been spending some of my spare time researching or doing math instead of coding in my productive time. i watch youtubers like michael penn, blackpenredpen, prime newtons, and a few others for math videos that give interesting problems. i also self study through my small textbook collection and pdfs of other books i find online. i love my ipad with goodnotes for this, it makes me feel so much more productive and portable with my learning and makes math so much easier to actually do. right now i'm studying real analysis, but i feel also drawn to number theory, measure theory, probability, and a few other fields. i think im drawn to math because to me, it's the programming language of the universe, which just feels natural to study after studying programming languages of computers. if i want to learn something scientific that matters outside of the digital world, i feel like math and physics are the best two things to study after learning coding so well.

physics | updated 9/15/2024

physics feels like the program written in math to me, but with all of its weird quirks its also something that needs to be studied. science isn't purely rational after all, and the evidence often contradicts mathematical logic or is needed to supplant mathematical logic. i also feel like physics is the lowest level science and the most intuitive, while chemistry feels like applied physics and makes little sense to me outside of physical chemistry with how many exceptions to general rules there are. i've only taken one physics class formally, but i self study through textbooks i own(notably, i'm studying quantum mechanics through griffith's 3rd edition introduction to quantum mechanics.

history/politics | updated 9/15/2024

studying politics feels necessary both to me as a trans woman living in the united states and as an academic. as a trans woman, its important for me to be aware of the current political climate but more importantly what support is effective, how to support myself, how not to support myself, how to spot people trying to use my identity for credibility(such as neoliberal queer rights proponents), etc. as an academic, i feel too limited in just knowing math and physics, which are largely inapplicable to most people today besides the products they use. i think politics is far more practical to study, and gives me more purpose in the world. also, politics(notably marxist political theory) is far more scientific than the general public is informed of, and so i want to study the science of politics to find for myself what i believe in, agree with, and what works best for everyone. i have more about this in philosophy and quotes, but i mostly read marxist literature such as marx(obviously), engels, lenin, stalin, mao, but some others such as angela davis, web dubois, etc.

programming
